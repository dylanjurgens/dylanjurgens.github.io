Does Nebraska football have an alternate uniform curse?

Author

Dylan Jurgens

Published

May 5, 2024

The Nebraska Cornhuskers football team has a storied history. Most of the time, people think of the past when talking about the Huskers’ football legacy that is still stuck in the 90s. Nebraska football uniform history is also pretty storied as well.

The Huskers’ alternate uniform history dates back to the 1966 Orange Bowl against the Alabama Crimson Tide. Nebraska decided to go away from their traditional white jersey and red pants look to an all-white look. Yes, this is when the dreaded all-white jersey originated from. The Huskers would go on and lose 33-7 to the Crimson Tide. There are two main types of alternate uniform Nebraska has worn in it’s history. You have the traditional alternate uniform that is a one-time use. The second type is the all-white with the traditional look.

After the 1967 Orange Bowl, the Huskers didn’t wear all-white unifroms until the 1991 Citrus Bowl against the No. 2 Georgia Tech Yellow Jackets. They thought this would be a good idea because it’s been a while since we wore all-white, let’s try it now. Well, things did not go well as the Yellow Jackets demolished the Huskers 45-21.

The first time with this happening was in the 1974 Cotton Bowl against the Texas Longhorns. The Huskers wore white jerseys with red pants, but the thing that was changed was there was a bold “Nebraska” across the top of the jersey. Nebraska has worn many different types of alternate uniforms like a Blackshirt uniforms, all black uniforms, all red uniforms, throwback uniforms, etc. Starting in 2012, Adidas and the Huskers started an annual alternate uniform game, which has gone both well and horribly for Nebraska.

updatedregular <- Updatedseasons |>
  filter(Uniforms=="Regular") |>
  summarise(
    avg_pd = mean(pd)
  ) |>
  mutate(
    Uniforms = "Regular"
  )

updatedallwhite <- Updatedseasons |>
  filter(Uniforms=="All-White") |>
  summarise(
    avg_pd = mean(pd)
  ) |>
  mutate(
    Uniforms = "All-White"
  )

updatedalternate <- Updatedseasons |>
  filter(Uniforms=="Alternate") |>
  select(Uniforms, pd) |>
  summarise(
    avg_pd = mean(pd)

  mutate(
    Uniforms = "Alternate"
  )

Now with each alternate uniform, there has been an absolute meltdown in the specific game that was played. Starting with the 2013 week three match up against the No. 16 UCLA Bruins. The Huskers were coming off two wins over the Wyoming Cowboys and Southern Mississippi Golden Eagles. Nebraska held a 21-3 lead over the Bruins in the second quarter before a monumental collapse by the Huskers.

Nebraska scored it’s last points on a 22-yard touchdown reception by Kenny Bell. UCLA turned around and scored 38 unanswered points to clinch a 41-21 victory over the Huskers.
